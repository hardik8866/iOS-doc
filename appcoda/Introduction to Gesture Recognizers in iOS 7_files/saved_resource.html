          Hi Gabriel. Was very interested in how to use the Swipe implementation. Works great! Though, I have a question on how to keep the subViews to match the widths of the superViews (relevant for the new phone sizes today).

          That is, using AutoLayout and Storyboard, I created constraints for the three subViews to 'Equal' the 'Width' of the superView. However, when running, only the subView that is in the center (default onscreen) matches the width of the superView. The two subViews offscreen maintain the widths that they were created with in the Storyboard.

          Note: Just to test, I moved one of the subViews onto the screen (so there were two subViews onscreen, one offscreen), and BOTH of the subViews matched the width of the superView while the one offscreen did not. Performed on the iPhone 6 simulator with inferred simulator metrics of iPhone 4-inch (320 wide).

              Just kidding. Found my problem. The UIViews off screen were each only -320 and +320; where for an iPhone 6, for example, the width of the screen is 375. My fix: attach horizontal spacing constraints between the UIViews to keep them strict neighbors.
